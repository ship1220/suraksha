FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

  • 1. How can I contact the Mumbai Fire Brigade in case of an emergency?

    Answer:You can contact the Mumbai Fire Brigade by dialing the emergency number 101.

  • 2. What should I do if I smell gas in my building?

    Answer: If you smell gas, immediately open windows and doors to ventilate the area, avoid using electrical switches or open flames, and call the Mumbai Fire Brigade at 101.

  • 3. What is the procedure for getting a fire safety inspection for my building?

    Answer: To get a fire safety inspection, you need to submit an application to the Mumbai Fire Brigade. The application can be submitted online or at the nearest fire station. Once submitted, an inspection will be scheduled, and the fire safety officer will conduct the inspection.

  • 4. How can I get a fire no-objection certificate (NOC) for my building?

    Answer: To obtain a fire NOC, you need to apply to the Mumbai Fire Brigade with all required documents, including building plans, fire safety equipment details, and compliance reports. After reviewing the application and conducting an inspection, the NOC will be issued if all fire safety norms are met.

  • 5. What are the common fire safety equipment required for residential buildings?

    Answer: Common fire safety equipment for residential buildings includes fire extinguishers, smoke detectors, fire alarms, hose reels, and emergency exit signs.

  • 6. How often should fire drills be conducted in commercial buildings?

    Answer: Fire drills should be conducted at least twice a year in commercial buildings to ensure that all occupants are familiar with evacuation procedures and fire safety measures.

  • 7. What should I do if I witness a fire accident?

    Answer: If you witness a fire accident, immediately call the Mumbai Fire Brigade at 101, provide the location and details of the fire, and try to assist in evacuating people from the area if it is safe to do so.

  • 8. Are there any fire safety guidelines for using electrical appliances at home?

    Answer: Yes, fire safety guidelines for using electrical appliances at home include not overloading electrical circuits, using certified appliances, unplugging devices when not in use, and regularly checking for damaged cords or faulty wiring.

  • 9. How can I become a firefighter with the Mumbai Fire Brigade?

    Answer: To become a firefighter with the Mumbai Fire Brigade, you need to meet the eligibility criteria, which typically include age, educational qualifications, and physical fitness standards. You must then pass a written examination, physical tests, and an interview process.

  • 10. What measures should be taken to prevent fire hazards in the kitchen?

    Answer: To prevent fire hazards in the kitchen, keep flammable items away from the stove, never leave cooking unattended, keep a fire extinguisher nearby, and ensure all appliances are turned off after use.

  • 11. How can industries ensure compliance with fire safety regulations?

    Answer: Industries can ensure compliance with fire safety regulations by conducting regular fire safety audits, installing and maintaining appropriate fire safety equipment, training employees on fire safety protocols, and obtaining necessary fire safety certifications.

  • 12. What should I do if a fire extinguisher is not working during an emergency?

    Answer: If a fire extinguisher is not working during an emergency, evacuate the area immediately, close doors behind you to contain the fire, and call the Mumbai Fire Brigade at 101.

  • 13. How can I report a fire safety violation in my area?

    Answer: To report a fire safety violation, you can contact the Mumbai Fire Brigade through their helpline number 101 or visit the nearest fire station to file a complaint.

  • 14. What are the fire safety precautions for high-rise buildings?

    Answer: Fire safety precautions for high-rise buildings include installing sprinkler systems, ensuring clear and accessible fire exits, conducting regular fire drills, and having fire alarms and smoke detectors on every floor.

  • 15. Can I get fire safety training from the Mumbai Fire Brigade?

    Answer: Yes, the Mumbai Fire Brigade offers fire safety training programs for individuals, organizations, and schools. You can contact your nearest fire station to inquire about available training sessions and schedules.
